# Trait Intelligence Framework

## Why traits matter

Skills show whether a candidate can perform tasks. Traits show whether the candidate is likely to sustain performance, handle pressure, align with the role, and improve over time.

## Evaluation principles

Trait scoring must be:

- Role-specific
- Evidence-based
- Scenario-driven
- Auditable
- Free from protected-attribute inference
- Reviewed by humans before final employment decision

## Operations Manager blueprint

| Trait | Weight | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| Ownership | 15 | Owns SLA, quality, productivity, staffing, and recovery plans |
| Execution Discipline | 15 | Runs daily review rhythm, trackers, escalations, and closure governance |
| Problem Solving | 15 | Uses data, root cause analysis, and prevention controls |
| People Leadership | 12 | Coaches TLs/agents, handles conflict, attrition, and performance improvement |
| Accountability | 12 | Accepts responsibility without blame and creates corrective action |
| Client Centricity | 10 | Handles client escalations transparently with recovery commitments |
| Integrity | 10 | Refuses data manipulation and protects compliance |
| Adaptability | 6 | Handles volume spikes, process change, and ambiguity |
| Role Alignment | 5 | Understands the actual responsibility of the operations manager role |

## Evidence sources

1. Scenario questions
2. Behavioral examples
3. Work simulations
4. Role alignment response
5. Interview validation
6. Post-hire performance feedback in future releases
